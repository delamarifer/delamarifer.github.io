How do Gradient Backgrounds affect the Perception of a Motion-Enhanced Opaque Square?

Alicia Chen, Emelie Eldracher, Nnamdi Obi

We investigate the simultaneous brightness contrast by examining how our gradient perception changes with the movement of two overlaid squares. By harnessing elements of motion, spacing, and color, we examine the gradient appearance on the smaller foregrounded square. It is that front square that contains no official gradient, yet one seems to materialize. By including motion, we analyze when the gradient is the most visible. We’ve included multiple variations of this illusion to try to break the illusion and to test its limits. By utilizing color, we further compare the perception of the grayscale and its equally gradient color counterpart. Please take our quiz to see how you match up against our illusion.
