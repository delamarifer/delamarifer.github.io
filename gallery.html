Visual illusion lab

Spring 2023 Class Gallery

Helpful tip: try zooming your page in and out to find an optimal viewing size for each illusion.

1. Does the Thatcher illusion (and the face inversion effect) hold in scenes other than human faces? This can help us investigate the features that are salient to make a face/scene look like itself. In the human face case, we can see the eyes, nose, and mouths dictate the illusion. How does that extend to non-human faces?
2. Does contrast or the amount of disparity between eyes affect our ability to fuse and perceive 3D images?
3. How do color, shape, and illumination work collectively or against each other in Vasarely's 'Nested Squares' Illusion?
4. How do color-based grouping cues affect the relatability of Gabor filter contours?
5. How Do We Interpret Depth When Different Combinations of Cues Conflict?
6. Exploring the limit of contour continuation with differently shaped and sized occluders
7. Exploring the Effects of Background Gradient, Hue Opponency, and Boundary Shape on Simultaneous Contrast Perception
8. To what extent are 2-dimensional cues important for perception of motion, and are motion illusions preserved when 1-dimensional cues are manipulated?
9. Can strong evidence that conflicts with priors of light sources coming from above affect the inference of illumination?
10. Do results similar to that of the horizontal and vertical McCollough effect occur with diagonal gratings of various orientations?
11. Animated Depth Perception with Modal Completion
12. Does an inverted shadow still provide motion cues?
