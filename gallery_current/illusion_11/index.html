Animated Depth Perception with Modal Completion

Ariba Khan, Steven Raphael

This illusion we wished to create an animation that gives the illusion of a 3D modal completion.

Modal completion is a phenomenon where our brain perceives a visible region of a scene as a separate object. Modal completion may influence depth perception as illusory contours in modal completion may provide additional information that our brain can use to infer depth in a scene. In particular, we were inspired by the abilities of mid-level vision as it involves the processing and interpretation of visual information to create a coherent perception of the scene and is respondible for both modal completion and depth perception.

In this case, the animation consists of two sets slightly offset horizontal lines moving in a sinusoidal pattern, which gives the impression of a 3D structure.

Our question was: Can we take advantage of mid-level vision process in order to create the experience of modal completion in an animation with a set of sinusoidally moving horizontal lines?

With regards to our scientific question, we wish to check four conditions in which a variable is changed in order to see the constraints of the phenomena.

1. Varying the size between the two sets of lines (set space)

2. Varying the number of lines within each set (num lines)

3. Varying the spacing between the lines (line space)

4. Occluding the outer edges of the lines.

Note: It is okay if you can not see the 3D percept, just depends on the person and time!

Now that you have watched through all the illusion experiments, it is clearer to see that some of the conditions degrade the illusions while others maintain it. For instance, the animation with sparser lines degrades the illusion since with fewer lines and larger gaps between them, the visual system receives less integrated visual information, making it harder to "fill in the gap" and percieve depth. Similarly, by occluding the outer edges, and keep all other variables the same as the original, the 3D depth perception looks more like a moving snake!
