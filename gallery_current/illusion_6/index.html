Exploring the limit of contour continuation with differently shaped and sized occluders

Sami Amer, Simon Radhakrishnan, Og Okunbo

Our illusion attempts to understand the relation between the size of the occluder and the continuous quality of the contour.

Our illusion design consists of 2 contours; one relatable contour and one unrelatable contour. A relatable contour is one that generally aims towards the same position and similar angle behind the occluder, making it more likely for someone to perceive the contour as continuous behind the occluder. In contrast, a non-relatable contour introduces inflection points along the contour, breaking this illusion and creating the perception of two separate contours behind the occluder.

To test this idea, we placed 3 different occluders – a circle, a rectangle, and a star – over the relatable and non-relatable contours. For each video, we start at a large size for each occluder, and gradually shrink the occluder until the entire contour is clearly visible. In doing so, we aim to measure the point at which the viewer is able to see the contour as a continuous contour. In addition, we decided to further test this concept by including illusions where the occluders were the same colour as the white background instead of the standard grey to see if the colour of the occluders affected the continuity and relatability of the contours.

Instructions: For each of the following videos, watch the video and note at which timestamp (second) the contour begins to appear continuous. Because the progress bar is pretty large, feel free to estimate if to the half second (e.g. if the contour appears continuous after the 1 second mark but before the 2 second mark, feel free to say 1.5 seconds)
